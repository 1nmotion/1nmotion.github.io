An Education

Chances are you won't understand these MR AND MRS (39-Down) if you're not a KingsWay student. Of course, they describe four of the staff there. See, for, example Mr and Mrs—take a half of—"Strengthening construction material" (64-Across), STEEL BEAM—forming Science and Chemistry teacher Mrs Steel (and her husband).

The worst clue was obviously "Amazon or Congo?..." (52-Across) I had no idea how to clue FORREST into the grid as it's spelt incorrectly, but I had to settle nonetheless. The other half of the clue reads as RAIN FOR REST. Horrible. I know. I wanted to do something with Mrs Price, such as RETAIL PRICE, but the theme revealer (39-Down) wouldn't be able to fit anywhere so whatever.

The crossword also has a lot of proper noun fills, many names like CARA, ALAN, CHLOE, ERIC, TAYS, GAGA, STREISAND, ROALD and of course amazing Amy—AMY ADAMS that is. "Hospitality teacher" (70-Across) was quite great as IRMA. These really solidified the very name-y topic of the puzzle.

The puzzle was constructed on this new software I found, Ingrid, and it was quite helpful in helping me fill in the fill. Some of my favourite fill answers/clues are listed below.

12-Down: Language is a beautiful thing... and "Place for extras?", I think, is quite beautiful. "Extra" here refers very much to the film nobodies background-ing, and their place in a MOVIE.
4-Down: "Procedor of technology" could've been quite misleading, though but it's really alluding to a common prefix to technology... NANO.
3-Down: Opting for picture clues is always fun, and hopefully also fun for the solvers. The image pasted is GRUMPY CAT, an internet celebrity, with many other animals, turned into 25-Down: MEMES.
5-Down: Bad word, probably. The only reason I know this is because of BTD6, one of the path upgrades for the Ninja Monkey. The answer was CALTROP.
40-Down: Did y'all like this? I thought it was really nice. You might be expecting "Mirror image" to be some sort of clone or lookalike, but in a more literal sense, you would really be seeing your SELF in a mirror.

Thank you for solving, and happy solving for the rest of my puzzles!
